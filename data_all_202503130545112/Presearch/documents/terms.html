Token Types

The Presearch Token (PRE) is the fundamental unit of value within the Presearch platform. PRE are pre-paid advertising credits for use on the platform. Members can both purchase PRE and earn PRE through their contributions on the platform, as well as transfer PRE credits to and from supported public blockchains.

PRE tokens in a Member's account can be found in the following locations:

PRE Wallet: The tokens in the PRE Wallet belong to the Member and are held on the Presearch platform for safekeeping. All deposits, purchases, transfers, claimed rewards, and removed token stakes go into this wallet. When tokens are staked, the balance of this wallet is reduced.

Staking: These tokens have been pledged to a particular purpose, and belong to the Member so long as they fulfill that purpose without violating the Terms of Service. Staking is a mechanism enabling Members to have something at stake (“skin in the game”), since they are liable to forfeit their stake if they participate in any behavior that violates the Presearch Terms of Service. By having something staked, this enables Presearch to have higher levels of trust in a Member, and therefore to often provide higher levels of rewards or other benefits to the Member.

If a Member violates the Terms of Service, however, then tokens staked by that Member can be revoked. As an example, if a Member stakes PRE on a keyword advertisement and submits a malicious ad, their staked PRE may be forfeited and not recoverable. If a node operator stakes PRE on a node, and then modifies the node software, tries to capture network data, or otherwise intentionally causes network disruptions, their staked PRE may be forfeited and not recoverable. If a Member stakes PRE toward search staking and then is determined by our fraud detection system to have generated invalid search or referral activity, then their staked tokens may be forfeited and not recoverable.

Rewards: These tokens do not belong to the Member until the Member has claimed them on the platform and transferred them to their PRE Wallet. Some rewards tokens (such as Usage Rewards) require obtaining a minimum rewards balance before the tokens can be claimed. Other rewards tokens (like Node Rewards) may have no minimum balance requirement.

Token Time Locks

In order to prevent abuse and arbitrage opportunities across the Presearch network, a 24 hour lock is placed on withdrawals of any tokens that are:

Unstaked: This timelock prevents a Member from staking tokens, performing a malicious act on the network, and then immediately unstaking and withdrawing their tokens before the abuse or exploit is caught.

Purchases: This timelock prevents Members from using the Presearch platform for market arbitrage opportunities versus external markets.

Reward Claims: This timelock allows fraud detection systems time to detect abuse or inappropriately-earned rewards associated with a rewards claim.

Time locks only affect withdrawals from your PRE Wallet - they do not affect your token balances or ability to immediately stake your tokens on the platform. To withdraw tokens, though, you must always hold your unstaked, claimed, or purchased in your PRE Wallet for at least 24 hours before attempting to withdrawal them.

NOTE: There is no time lock on deposits into your PRE Wallet.

Rewards and Withdrawals

Before a Presearch Member can use their Presearch Reward Tokens or withdraw them to the blockchain, they must first earn enough tokens to claim them and transfer them to their PRE Wallet. Claiming of PRE Rewards tokens is subject to the following limitations:

For Usage Rewards:

  • 1,000 PRE must be earned in order to claim Usage Rewards
  • A maximum of 1,000 PRE may be claimed every 90 days
  • Presearch reserves the right to increase or decrease these thresholds at any time

For Node Rewards:

  • Any amount of PRE rewards may be claimed at any time

Once a user has claimed their rewards tokens, they own those tokens within their PRE Wallet, at which point the member may hold them, stake them, or request a withdrawal.

Once a withdrawal request is initiated, one of two things will occur:

  1. It will be automatically approved for an immediate withdrawal by the Member to their digital wallet (i.e. an Ethereum wallet that supports ERC-20 tokens).
  2. It will be put in a queue to be approved by a Presearch administrator. This scenario may occur if the withdrawal amount is high enough to trigger a manual review, if a significant number of withdrawals across the platform have triggered a temporary manual review of all transactions, for an occasional randomized review, if there is a potential anomaly (fraud, abuse, security issue, etc.) with the Member's account that warrants further review prior to completing the withdrawal transaction. If a request has been issued or otherwise allowed involving a system error or malicious activity, Presearch reserves the right to block a request at the approval stage and/or rescind any staked or previously rewarded tokens. If your request is initially denied for any reason, you will be notified by a message on the site and will be directed to contact support@presearch.io.

You may be limited to a certain number of withdrawals in a certain period of time, or a certain number of tokens at a single time or in a period of time. This number may change based upon your account activity, or based on the needs of the project.

If your account is left dormant for more than one year, meaning that you do not login and record a search for 365 days, Presearch will consider your account abandoned and will rescind any unclaimed Rewards tokens in your account. This does not apply to any tokens that have been purchased from Presearch, previously claimed, staked by the Member, or transferred onto the platform from the blockchain.
